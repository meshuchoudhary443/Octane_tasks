BENIFITS OF YOGA PRACTICES
1. PHYSICAL HEALTH BENIFITS


Yoga offers a myriad of physical health benefits that contribute to overall well-being. Through regular practice of yoga poses (asanas), individuals can significantly improve their physical fitness. Yoga enhances flexibility by stretching muscles and increasing joint range of motion, which not only helps prevent injuries but also promotes better posture and alignment. Moreover, the strength-building aspects of yoga poses, such as holding poses and balancing, help tone muscles and improve core strength. This increased strength and flexibility can lead to better physical performance in various activities and reduce the risk of chronic conditions related to muscle and joint stiffness. Additionally, yoga fosters better circulation and enhances cardiovascular health, supporting overall vitality and endurance. These combined benefits make yoga a valuable practice for improving physical health and maintaining a balanced body.

2. MENTAL HELTH BENIFITS

Yoga offers numerous mental health benefits that can significantly enhance overall well-being. Regular practice of yoga, which often includes meditation and breathing techniques, has been shown to reduce stress and promote relaxation. Through deep breathing exercises (pranayama), yoga helps calm the nervous system, lowering cortisol levels and inducing a state of relaxation that counters the effects of chronic stress. This can lead to improved mood and decreased symptoms of anxiety and depression. Furthermore, yoga promotes mindfulness, which involves focusing on the present moment without judgment. Mindfulness practices during yoga sessions cultivate self-awareness and emotional resilience, helping individuals manage challenging emotions more effectively. This heightened awareness can also improve concentration and cognitive function, enhancing mental clarity and decision-making abilities. Moreover, the physical aspects of yoga, such as the stretching and strengthening of muscles, release tension and contribute to a sense of physical well-being, which in turn supports mental health. The combination of physical movement, breathing exercises, and mindfulness practices in yoga creates a holistic approach to mental health that fosters a positive outlook and emotional balance. Overall, yoga provides a comprehensive toolkit for improving mental health by reducing stress, promoting relaxation, enhancing mindfulness, and supporting emotional resilience. Incorporating yoga into one's routine can contribute to a healthier mind and a more balanced life.

SOME BEGINNER-FRIENDLY YOGA POSES

1.Downward facing dog(ADHO MUKHA SVANASANA)

Place your palms underneath your shoulders and keep your legs bent at 90 degrees in an all-four position on the mat. Initiate the pose by pressing into your palms, curling your toes under, and lifting your hips high into the sky. Press your heels toward the ground and feel a big stretch in your upper back and down the back of your legs. Gently pedal your feet one at a time to enhance the stretch.

2.MOUNTAIN POSE(TADAASANA )

Stand at the top of your yoga mat and bring your feet together to touch. Actively separate your fingers and gently move your arms about 5 inches away from your waist. Stand tall, feel your feet on the ground beneath you, inhale through your nose, and take a nice long exhale through your mouth.

3.Triangle Pose (Utthita Trikoṇāsana)

Take a wide stance with your feet and position your front foot forward and your back foot perpendicular. Keeping yourself balanced, bring your left hand to your front foot. Rotate your torso so your right arm is now pointing toward the sky, and be sure to look toward your hand. Take several deep breaths and maintain your balance as you hold the pose.

4.Cat-Cow (Bidalasana)

Get yourself into an all-four position on the mat with your spine neutral and your eyes facing the mat beneath you. Take a big breath in and extend your back and neck, looking toward the sky above you. On the exhale, bend your spine and neck into a C-curve and look back toward the mat beneath you.
